WHY NOW
PRD-252 decoupled the continuation path onto
`CONTINUATION_MAX_RISK_PCT_PER_TRADE=0.01` "until its ATR proxy's
max-loss understatement is fixed" (2026-07-10); no PRD carries that fix,
and `docs/PROJECT_STATE.md`'s known-debt entry re-evaluates 2026-08-15.
As it stands, "temporary decouple" is indistinguishable from "two
permanent risk caps" — and a wrong max-loss number is a wrong
invalidation level, one of the four questions this system exists to
pre-answer. Exposure is bounded by the $150 cap today; this is a queued
slot, not an incident.

MAX EXPECTED DELTA
Phase 1 (R1, always in scope): 1 file
(`PRD-251.continuation-path.proposal.md`), no code. R2 (the ruling, a
doc-only gate) adds up to 2 further doc files. Phase 2 (R3 or R4,
mutually exclusive, chosen at R2, never both): each touches at most 6

GOAL
Replace the current ambiguity — is the continuation path's frozen $150
budget temporary or permanent? — with a recorded, evidenced ruling made
AFTER the evidence exists: characterize the ATR proxy's max-loss
understatement first (Phase 1), rule on the characterization (R2), then
either fix and re-converge onto the raised ~$400 budget or declare the
two-cap split permanent and strike "temporary" language everywhere it
appears (Phase 2). Both outcomes are acceptable; ruling before Phase 1
exists, or staying silent, is not. This PRD's own Gate A approves only
the two-phase shape and Phase 1's scope, NOT the FIX-vs-PERMANENT choice
— that choice is R2, a separate gate that fires after Phase 1's analysis
is actually in hand, not a decision Gate A pre-commits to on no data.

REQUIREMENTS

R1 — Phase 1: characterize the understatement
Produce a quantified comparison, across a representative ATR14 range and
both CREDIT and DEBIT strategy resolutions, of what the continuation
path actually charges against real max loss. This requires tracing the
full two-layer path, not `_qualify_continuation_candidate`'s proxy in
isolation:
- **Layer 1** (`_qualify_continuation_candidate`,
  `spread_width = max(0.50, atr14 * 0.05)`) computes an ATR-scaled
  `QualificationResult.dollar_risk`/`.max_contracts` — but this figure is
  discarded before rendering; it survives only as one operand of a
  contract-count cap.
- **Layer 2** (`options.py::build_option_setups`) re-sizes every
  continuation result from a fixed, ATR-independent figure
  (`_estimated_debit(strike_distance) = 0.30 * strike_distance`),
  deliberately excluding continuation results from the strategy-aware
  `_max_loss_for_strategy` correction PRD-251 applies to every other
  candidate. This is the layer whose output (via `TradeDecision`,
  confirmed a pure passthrough, never a further rescale) actually reaches
  the rendered and audited record.
The quantified comparison is therefore: layer 2's charged figure vs. what
`_max_loss_for_strategy` would give for the same strategy — real max-loss
= width - credit for CREDIT strategies (`BULL_PUT_SPREAD`); DEBIT
strategies (`BULL_CALL_SPREAD`) price at the charged figure directly, no
gap. Append to `docs/prd_history/PRD-251.continuation-path.proposal.md`.
Deliverable is the analysis only — no recommendation, no pre-committed
direction.

FAIL: no quantified comparison exists, it covers only one strategy type,
it characterizes layer 1 in isolation without tracing whether that
figure reaches the rendered/audited record, or it states a
FIX/PERMANENT recommendation instead of leaving the ruling to R2.

FAIL: R3 or R4 work begins before R1's analysis is implemented AND a

**RULED: FIX (2026-07-13).** Recorded in `docs/DECISIONS.md`'s
2026-07-13 R2 ruling entry. Grounds (full text in DECISIONS.md): the
continuation path does not size off an inaccurate estimate — layer 2
(`options.py::build_option_setups`) deliberately excludes continuation
results from the strategy-aware correction PRD-251 applies to every
other candidate, understating CREDIT max loss by a flat 2.333x, always.
There is no honest PERMANENT branch: ruling PERMANENT would mean writing
into the record that this system understates credit-strategy max loss by
design, which it is not — it is a bypass, not a conservative estimate.
Phase 2 (R3) is ruled but not yet authorized; it gets its own dispatch.
