Fast-follow proposal — continuation-path credit-spread max-loss fix

Status: PROPOSAL — not started, no Gate A yet. Not a registered PRD (this
is a `.proposal.md` sidecar, allowlisted from the registry-gap check per
PRD-248; it deliberately carries no `docs/PRD_REGISTRY.md` row and claims
no PRD number until Dustin gives it a Gate A direction ruling).

ORIGIN
Surfaced during PRD-251 (A1a)'s Stage 0 sweep, 2026-07-10. PRD-251 fixes
credit-spread max-loss arithmetic on the standard Gate 8 path
(`cuttingboard/qualification.py::qualify_candidate`, the 11-gate flow).
This proposal is the same conceptual defect, reached through a second,
structurally independent door.

THE FINDING
`cuttingboard/qualification.py::_qualify_continuation_candidate` (lines
630-730) qualifies EXPANSION-regime continuation candidates via its own
sizing block (line 707: `spread_width = max(0.50, dm.atr14 * 0.05)`) —
an ATR-scaled debit estimate added in PRD-008 (commit 30b583a),
structurally unrelated to the strike-distance-based 30%-of-width
convention `cuttingboard/options.py` uses for the standard path.

This candidate's `direction` is hardcoded `"LONG"` (line 726). Its
resulting `QualificationResult` flows into
`cuttingboard/options.py::build_option_setups`, which calls
`_select_strategy(result.direction, sr.iv_environment)` (options.py:183)
the same way it does for every other candidate — so in ELEVATED_IV or
HIGH_IV, a continuation candidate becomes `BULL_PUT_SPREAD`, a CREDIT
strategy. Its qualify/reject decision and its dollar_risk, though, were
computed by the ATR proxy above, under the same latent assumption
PRD-251 is fixing elsewhere: that the estimated premium collected IS the
max loss, rather than max loss = width - premium.

WHY THIS IS NOT A DROP-IN OF PRD-251's FIX
PRD-251's correction (max loss = width - credit, i.e. 70% of width) is
computable directly because `width` (strike_distance: $5 ETF / $2.50
stock, `cuttingboard/options.py::_MAX_STRIKE_DIST_ETF`/`_STK`) is a real,
known quantity at the Gate 8 call site. The continuation path's ATR
proxy has NO such width term — it's a dollar value derived straight from
ATR14, with no strike-distance concept anywhere in its computation or
its PRD-008 history/comments. Applying "width - credit" here requires
first deciding what "width" MEANS for a continuation trade. That is a
modeling decision, not an arithmetic correction, and per
`audits/EXECUTION_DOCTRINE.md`'s HARD CONSTRAINT for this slice ("if the
continuation-path fix needs materially different logic... HALT and
report before building — that's evidence it deserves its own slice"),
Dustin ruled to descope it (2026-07-10, see docs/DECISIONS.md) rather
than fold it into PRD-251.

OPEN DESIGN QUESTION (needs its own Gate A before any build)
What should "width" mean for an ATR-based continuation candidate, so its
credit-strategy outcomes get a real max-loss correction? Candidate
options surfaced so far (not evaluated, not ranked):

1. Borrow options.py's existing strike-distance buckets ($5 ETF / $2.50
   stock) as the implied width for continuation candidates too; treat
   the current ATR-based value as the credit estimate; apply
   max_loss = width - credit the same shape as PRD-251, but now
   cross-referencing a constant from a different subsystem's convention
   that wasn't designed with continuation trades in mind.
2. A continuation-native width derivation (e.g. scale the implied width
   off ATR itself, independent of the ETF/stock buckets) — invents a new
   convention rather than reusing an existing one.
3. Leave the continuation path's proxy as a debit-only estimate and
   force continuation candidates that land on a credit strategy through
   a different, more conservative path (e.g. always MANUAL_CHECK or
   reject) rather than sizing them at all until real economics are
   known — sidesteps the modeling question by refusing to price it.

None of these is picked. This needs Dustin's direction before Stage 0 of
whatever PRD number gets assigned to it.

## PRD-256 R1 — characterization (2026-07-12)

Deliverable is the quantified analysis only. It states no FIX/PERMANENT
recommendation and pre-commits to no direction — that ruling is R2's, fired
separately after Dustin reads this section.

### 1. How the proxy estimates max loss today

`cuttingboard/qualification.py::_qualify_continuation_candidate`, lines
707-733:

```
spread_width = max(0.50, dm.atr14 * 0.05)
spread_cost = spread_width * 100
continuation_budget = config.ACCOUNT_EQUITY * config.CONTINUATION_MAX_RISK_PCT_PER_TRADE
max_contracts = floor(continuation_budget / spread_cost)
dollar_risk = max_contracts * spread_cost
```

`spread_width` is a pure function of `dm.atr14` (5% of the real, Wilder-RMA
ATR14, floored at $0.50/share) — it has no strike-distance, width, DTE, or
moneyness term anywhere in its computation, confirming the proposal doc's
original finding. This same dollar figure is used, unconditionally, as BOTH
the estimated premium AND the max loss charged against the $150 budget,
for every strategy the candidate can resolve into. Direction is hardcoded
`"LONG"` (line 742, in the `QualificationResult` this function returns);
`_select_strategy(direction, iv_environment)` in
`cuttingboard/options.py:300-316` then turns this into `BULL_CALL_SPREAD`
(debit, LOW_IV/NORMAL_IV) or `BULL_PUT_SPREAD` (credit, ELEVATED_IV/HIGH_IV)
downstream in `build_option_setups`.

### 3. DEBIT strategies: no structural gap

For `BULL_CALL_SPREAD` / `BEAR_PUT_SPREAD`, real max loss = debit paid.
The code already treats `spread_width` as the debit estimate and charges
it as the max loss — the two are definitionally identical regardless of
`dm.atr14`. Gap = $0.00 / 0% at every ATR14 reading tested. (Whether
`atr14 * 0.05` is an *accurate* debit estimate against a real option
chain is a separate, unverified magnitude question — no option-chain data
exists in this repo to check it — but there is no *structural*
credit-vs-max-loss conflation on this branch, unlike the direct path's
pre-PRD-251 bug.)

### 4. CREDIT strategies: quantified understatement

Real max loss for a credit spread = width - credit collected, the same
shape PRD-251 applied on the direct path
(`options.py::_max_loss_for_strategy`). Continuation candidates have no
width concept of their own (the proposal doc's open question); the only
concrete width convention anywhere in this codebase is the direct path's
strike-distance bucket (`_MAX_STRIKE_DIST_ETF=$5.00` /
`_MAX_STRIKE_DIST_STK=$2.50`). Using that bucket as a benchmark plug-in
for "width" — exactly the proposal doc's candidate option 1, used here
only to produce a number, not to endorse it as the fix — and treating
`spread_width` (the ATR proxy) as the credit-collected estimate (the same
role `_estimated_debit` plays on the direct path):

`real_max_loss = width - spread_width`

ascending; `*_ohlcv.parquet` as of 2026-07-10 cache refresh):

### 5. What drives the error, and which dimension dominates

- **Strategy type is the binary switch.** 100% of the understatement is
  on the CREDIT side (`BULL_PUT_SPREAD`/`BEAR_CALL_SPREAD`); DEBIT
  candidates carry zero structural gap by construction (section 3). This
  is a strict on/off partition, not a matter of degree.
- **ATR14 magnitude relative to the fixed-$ width bucket is the sole
  continuous driver within CREDIT candidates.** `spread_width` grows
  linearly with `atr14` while `width` is a fixed constant, so the gap
  shrinks as `atr14` rises and crosses zero at `atr14 = 10 x width`
  (derived: `width - 0.05*atr14 = 0.05*atr14` when the floor no longer
  binds) — $25 ATR14 for the $2.50 stock bucket, $50 for the $5.00 ETF
  bucket. `META`'s 24.744 sits just below its $25 crossover, matching the
  near-zero gap observed.
- **ETF-vs-stock bucket classification (`_INDEX_ETFS` membership) is a
  secondary, discrete driver** — it doubles both the width and the
  crossover point (IWM/SPY/QQQ vs everything else), which is why IWM and
  SPY show the largest percentage gaps in the table despite unremarkable
  ATR14 values.
- **DTE and moneyness are not inputs to this formula at all.** No
  expiration date or strike-distance/delta term appears anywhere in
  `_qualify_continuation_candidate`'s sizing block; `build_option_setups`
  picks a DTE for contract construction only after this dollar-risk
  figure is already fixed, so DTE never feeds back into the sizing
  decision.
- **Spread structure (put-side vs call-side) does not matter
  independently** — `BULL_PUT_SPREAD` and `BEAR_CALL_SPREAD` both resolve
  through identical arithmetic; only the credit/debit axis (strategy
  type, above) matters.

### 6. Bounded, or arbitrarily large?

Bounded above in dollar terms within the observed regime, structurally
undefined beyond it:

- While `spread_width` sits at its $0.50 floor (`atr14 <= 10`), the
  per-share gap is **constant** — $1.50 (stock bucket) or $4.00 (ETF
  bucket) — regardless of how low ATR14 goes; it does not grow without
  bound as volatility falls. 11 of 16 real tradable symbols are in this
  floor regime today (two more, `MSTR`/`COIN`, sit just past it with a
  nearly-as-large ratio — section 4).
- The gap narrows past `atr14 = 10` and crosses zero at the `10 x width`
  point above (section 5).
- Past `atr14 = 20 x width` ($50 stock / $100 ETF), `width - spread_width`
  goes **negative** — not "proxy overstates," but the borrowed
  strike-distance convention becomes mathematically undefined for a
  credit spread at that volatility (a spread can't have negative max
  loss). No currently-tradable symbol's real ATR14 reaches this (highest
  observed: META at 24.744, versus a 25 crossover / 50 breakdown point for
  its bucket). Two of the repo's cached symbols do cross this threshold —
  `GC=F` (atr14=88.8) and `BTC_USD` (atr14=2137.8) — but both are
  `config.NON_TRADABLE_SYMBOLS` (`MACRO_DRIVERS`), never routed through
  option qualification. This breakdown is therefore latent in the
  formula, not reachable in the live tradable universe today, and the
  code contains no explicit guard against it — worth carrying into
  whichever FIX approach R2 might choose, if one is chosen, since nothing
  currently prevents the tradable universe from growing to include a
  comparably high-ATR-dollar name.

### 7. Same direction as PRD-251's bug?

Yes, unambiguously, and by the identical mechanism. PRD-251's direct-path
bug treated the estimated credit collected as the max loss instead of
`width - credit`; DECISIONS.md's 2026-07-10 entry ruled that class of
issue (understating in the direction that lets more risk through)
warrants the decouple by default. Section 4's table shows exactly that
here: `spread_width` (today's proxy) plays the same structural role
`_estimated_debit` plays on the direct path, and for CREDIT strategies
the real max loss (`width - spread_width`) is 4.00x-9.00x what's charged
against the $150 budget in the common (floor) case — 3.7x-9.0x including
the two symbols (`MSTR`, `COIN`) sitting just past the floor. Confirmed,
not refuted — same class.

At today's actual $150 cap, a floor-regime CREDIT candidate (the common
case — 11/16 real symbols exactly at the floor, 13/16 including the two

multiplier from being applied to a larger budget. Near the ATR14
crossover (META), the gap collapses and the decouple's protective effect
is negligible — consistent with section 5's finding that ATR14 is the
dominant continuous driver.

### 9. Out-of-scope finding surfaced during real-data validation

Section 2's historical replay found the real, unmodified
`_qualify_continuation_candidate` never once clears `NO_HOLD_CONFIRMATION`
across ~4,000 real symbol-days, even with breakout firing 39/249 times on
NVDA alone in isolation. Cause: `detect_continuation_breakout`'s
`breakout_level` is the max High over the 5 bars *up to and including*
yesterday (`df.iloc[-(n+1):-1]` on the frame ending today); the hold check
then asks whether *yesterday's own close* already exceeded that same
window's max (which includes yesterday's own High) — a self-referential
comparison that is satisfiable only in the degenerate case where
yesterday closed exactly at its own high and that high was the window
maximum. This appears inconsistent with the documented spec
(`docs/trade_qualification.md:323`: "Close 1 completed bar ago also held
above the breakout level," implying an *earlier* breakout level than the
one the current bar just set). This is a candidate explanation for why
95/95 captured production runs and a full real-data historical replay
both show zero accepted continuation candidates ever. It is unrelated to
the ATR max-loss proxy PRD-256 characterizes and is **not fixed here** —
flagged for separate ticketing, not folded into this PRD's scope.
