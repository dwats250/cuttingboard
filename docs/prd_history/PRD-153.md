STATUS

WHY NOW
Phase 1 closed 2026-05-22 (alignment audit ALIGNED; Batch B
compatibility shim removal landed as PRD-152). VISION.md § Phase 2
opens with this work: build the read-only consumer that joins Moomoo
trade statements to `logs/audit.jsonl` so every trade Dustin takes can
be evaluated against the market state Cuttingboard observed at the
time.

MAX EXPECTED DELTA
~600 production LOC across three new modules in `cuttingboard/`
(`moomoo_parser.py`, `moomoo_join.py`, `moomoo_review.py`). One new
runtime dependency (`pdfplumber`). ~300 LOC of tests under `tests/`.
Exceeding ~750 production LOC requires amending this PRD with a
revised ceiling and reason.

GOAL
Deliver a standalone CLI that parses Moomoo client-statement PDFs into
normalized trade records, joins each trade to all same-day audit
records for its underlier symbol, and emits a per-trade JSONL log and
a per-statement Markdown report. Read-only, descriptive, non-predictive.

SCOPE
- A PDF parser that handles the Moomoo "Account Activity" table shape
  observed in the three local sample statements (Feb/Mar/Apr 2026):
  equity Buy/Sell, option Buy/Sell, Expired Option, FX Buy/Sell Trade,
  E-transfer Deposit, Cash Rebate, "Transactions to settle after
  current period" rows, Opening/Closing Balance rows.
- A normalizer that extracts the underlier symbol from option
  descriptions (e.g. `CALL 100 GLD 02/25/26 480` → underlier=`GLD`,
  strike=480, expiry=`2026-02-25`, right=`CALL`, contracts=1).
- A joiner that, for each trade with an underlier in Cuttingboard's
  universe, surfaces every audit record from the trade date matching
  that underlier, chronologically ordered.
- Blind-spot tagging on each enriched trade per
  `docs/DECISIONS.md` § Phase 2 scoping inputs (gap-down short
  suppression, notify-mode context loss, EXPANSION DATA_INCOMPLETE
  ambiguity).
- A CLI entry point `python -m cuttingboard.moomoo_review` that
  accepts a single PDF path or a directory of PDFs.
- JSONL output at `logs/moomoo_review.jsonl` (one record per trade).
- Markdown report at `reports/moomoo/<statement-period>.md`
  (per-statement, trades grouped by date).

OUT OF SCOPE
- Option pricing analysis (no IV, theta, strike-distance grading).
- Cross-statement performance aggregation (separate PRD downstream).
- Auto-discovery of new PDFs or scheduled invocation (manual CLI only).
- Renderer/dashboard integration; no Signal Forge surface changes.
- Modifications to `cuttingboard/runtime.py`, the audit writer, or any
  decision module. This PRD is a read-only consumer.
- Modifications to `cuttingboard/evaluation.py` or
  `cuttingboard/performance_engine.py`. The new consumer lives beside
  them, not inside them.
- Coverage of statement sections outside Account Activity (Asset
  Allocation, Holdings, Income Summary, disclosures). Future PRDs may
  consume those if needed.

REQUIREMENTS

R1 — Parser produces NormalizedTrade records from a Moomoo PDF
`cuttingboard.moomoo_parser.parse_statement(path: Path) -> list[NormalizedTrade]`
returns one `NormalizedTrade` per Account Activity row in either the
CAD or USD margin account section. Opening Balance, Closing Balance,
and the section header rows are skipped. "Transactions to settle after
current period" rows are parsed but tagged `next_period=True`. Each
record carries: `date` (datetime.date), `account`
(`"MARGIN_USD" | "MARGIN_CAD"`), `side`
(`"BUY" | "SELL" | "EXPIRED" | "FX_BUY" | "FX_SELL" | "DEPOSIT"`),
`instrument_class` (`"EQUITY" | "OPTION" | "FX" | "CASH"`),

Row-type enumeration: equity Buy/Sell, option Buy/Sell, Expired Option,
FX Buy/Sell Trade, E-transfer Deposit, Cash Rebate. The Cash Rebate row
type was added during 2026-05-22 real-statement validation (the Mar/Apr
statements contain ~30 rebate rows in the CAD section); rebates fold
into the existing CASH/DEPOSIT shape (1 trailing numeric, no underlier,
no option). Expired Option rows extract with TWO trailing numerics
(quantity + amount), not three — the Price column is empty for expired
contracts. Option descriptions may carry a trailing bare ``*`` token
between the strike and the quantity (e.g. ``CALL 100 GLD 02/25/26 480
* 1 5.000 (501.00)``); the ``*`` separator is part of the description's
rendered shape and must be tolerated, not parsed as a numeric.
E-transfer Deposit and similar wide Type cells wrap across two
physical text lines in the real PDFs (``"E-transfer\nDeposit"``); the
parser tolerates this via either a continuation pre-pass or by
recognising ``"E-transfer"`` as a single-token alias for the full
``"E-transfer Deposit"`` type. Multi-word equity descriptions without
a resolvable ticker (e.g. ``ETF OPPORTUNITIES TR T REX 2X``) yield
``underlier=None`` rather than guessing the first token; coverage loss
on those rows is preferable to false-negative blind-spot attribution
downstream.
`underlier` (str or None), `option` (OptionLeg or None), `quantity`
(float, signed), `price` (float or None), `amount` (float, signed),
`raw_description` (str, verbatim), `next_period` (bool).
`OptionLeg` carries `strike` (float), `expiry` (datetime.date),
`right` (`"CALL" | "PUT"`), `contracts` (int).

R2 — Joiner enriches trades with same-day audit context
`cuttingboard.moomoo_join.enrich(trades: list[NormalizedTrade],
audit_log_path: str = AUDIT_LOG_PATH) -> list[EnrichedTrade]` reads
`logs/audit.jsonl` (path import `from cuttingboard.audit import
AUDIT_LOG_PATH`) and, for each input trade, attaches:
- `audit_records` — all audit records whose `run_at_utc` date equals
  the trade date AND whose `qualified_trades`, `excluded_symbols`,
  `near_a_plus`, or `watchlist` mentions the trade's underlier.
  Records are ordered ascending by `run_at_utc`.
- `in_universe` — True iff `trade.underlier` is in
  `cuttingboard.config.INDICES + COMMODITIES + HIGH_BETA`.
- `blind_spots` — list of literal strings, possibly empty, drawn from
  the closed set {`"gap_down_short_suppressed"`, `"notify_mode_only"`,
  `"expansion_data_incomplete_ambiguous"`, `"no_audit_for_date"`,
  `"underlier_not_in_audit_universe"`}.

FAIL: `enrich` called against a fabricated audit-log fixture and a
fabricated trade list produces an `EnrichedTrade` whose
`audit_records` list, `in_universe` flag, and `blind_spots` list
match the values asserted in `tests/test_moomoo_join.py`. Any record
ordering inversion, missing match, or false-positive blind-spot
attachment fails the test.

R3 — Blind-spot attachment rules are deterministic
Within `enrich`:
- `"gap_down_short_suppressed"` is attached iff
  `trade.option.right == "PUT"` (or `trade.side == "SELL"` for
  equity short) AND the trade date appears in the audit log at all
  for some underlier, AND no audit record on that date mentions the
  trade's underlier in any candidate-or-rejection field. This is the
  conservative read of the gap-down-suppressed blind spot:
  invisibility in the audit cannot prove suppression, but for short
  trades on a known-active session it is the correct hedge.
- `"notify_mode_only"` is attached iff none of the joined audit
  records carry intraday context on the per-symbol entry for the
  trade's underlier. The canonical intraday-context fields are
  `downside_permission`, `intraday_state`, and
  `intraday_state_available`, populated by `cuttingboard/audit.py`
  (the per-record block at `audit.py:141-145` for `qualified_trades`
  and `audit.py:153-157` for `near_a_plus`) only when an
  `intraday_state_context` is passed by the caller. Notify-mode runs
  omit this context; daily-mode runs include it for surviving
  candidates. The heuristic: for the underlier's entry across all
  joined records, if no entry has `intraday_state_available` set,
  attach the flag. Implementation may refine this heuristic if a
  more direct mode-tag becomes available in the audit record schema,
  but must not invent a field that does not exist at HEAD.
- `"expansion_data_incomplete_ambiguous"` is attached iff any joined
  audit record has `regime == "EXPANSION"` AND the trade's underlier
  appears in `excluded_symbols` with reason
  `"DATA_INCOMPLETE"` on that record.
- `"no_audit_for_date"` is attached iff `audit_records == []` AND the
  trade date has no audit records at all in the joined log.
- `"underlier_not_in_audit_universe"` is attached iff
  `audit_records == []` AND the trade date HAS audit records but none
  mention the underlier in any field.

FAIL: Tests in `tests/test_moomoo_join.py` exercise each of the five

that fires when it should not (false positive), or fails to fire

R4 — CLI orchestrates and emits both output channels
`python -m cuttingboard.moomoo_review <path>` accepts either a single
`.pdf` file or a directory; in the directory case it processes every
`*.pdf` file in the directory (non-recursive). For each processed
statement:
- Appends one JSONL line per trade to `logs/moomoo_review.jsonl`. The
  JSONL schema is: top-level keys `trade` (the `NormalizedTrade`
  dict-form), `in_universe` (bool), `blind_spots` (list[str]),
  `audit_records` (list[dict]), `statement_path` (str),
  `processed_at_utc` (ISO 8601 UTC string).
- Writes a Markdown report to
  `reports/moomoo/<statement-period>.md` where `<statement-period>`
  is derived from the statement's "Period Ending" date as
  `YYYY-MM` (e.g. `2026-02.md` for the Feb 28, 2026 statement). If
  the file already exists, it is overwritten — Markdown is a
  rendered view, not an append-only log.
- Exits 0 on success. Exits 1 if no PDFs were processed or if every
  PDF failed to parse. Per-file parse failures are logged to stderr
  but do not abort the batch; a partial success still exits 0.

NOTES
- 2026-05-22: Real-statement validation against the three
  `private/moomoo/*.pdf` samples surfaced six parser-fidelity defects
  not anticipated by the original R1 wording: Expired Option rows have
  no Price column (2 trailing numerics, not 3); option descriptions
  carry a trailing bare ``*`` between strike and quantity; the
  ``E-transfer Deposit`` Type cell wraps across two text lines; Cash
  Rebate is a real row type not previously enumerated; multi-word
  equity descriptions without an exchange ticker were silently
  mis-attributed to the first description token. R1 was amended in
  the same arc to enumerate Cash Rebate and to describe the Expired
  Option / ``* N`` / Type-cell-wrap / ticker-less-equity shapes
  explicitly. The CLI, JSONL/Markdown writers, and join-and-blind-spot
  behavior were unaffected by the parser defects (every real PDF
  aborted before reaching those writers).
- This PRD's three named blind spots come from
  `docs/DECISIONS.md` § Phase 2 scoping inputs (2026-05-22). They are
  acknowledged in the data — not remediated. Remediation, if any,
  belongs to follow-up PRDs after Phase 2 has shipped descriptively.
- The decision to surface ALL same-day audit records (not just one
  representative) means the JSONL records are larger than a typical
  audit row. This is deliberate per the brainstorming conversation:
  intraday evolution is lossy to summarize.
- The CLI is intentionally manual. Auto-discovery and scheduled
  invocation are out of scope until Phase 2 has produced enough
  artifacts to know whether automation earns its keep.
