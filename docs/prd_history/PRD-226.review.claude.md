SUMMARY
PRD-226 corrects a real, reachable correctness defect on a HIGH-RISK consumer
surface: the level diagram anchored its "NOW" line and every `_pct` 0% reference
on the contract's planned entry (`trade_candidates[].entry`) while labelling it
NOW — a price that is not the current price (a computed FVG midpoint under
`ENTRY_MODE_PULLBACK_IMBALANCE`, and in every mode a different artifact than the
market_map current price, so it diverges on staleness). The fix makes the live
current price the required NOW anchor and 0% reference, and renders the contract
entry as a distinct amber ENTRY level that remains the PRD-223 risk-band top
edge. Requirements R1–R4 are each backed by an observable FAIL line; the new red
tests are mutation-verified (reverting the anchor to the contract entry fails
them). No REQUIRED edits; two non-blocking RECOMMENDED notes below.

None.

   and amber ENTRY line render at the same y and overlap; the label-declutter
   pass still separates the two text labels, so no information is lost, but the
   two coincident lines are visually indistinguishable. Consider noting this as
   accepted behavior (labels carry the distinction) or, if undesirable, a small
   y-nudge. Non-blocking; the current output is honest. FAIL (if adopted): the
   PRD does not state whether coincident NOW/ENTRY lines are accepted.
