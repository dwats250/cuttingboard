## Task A -- Independent risk-ratio recomputation

Classification: REPRODUCED.

Echo exercised the real, unmodified runtime across:

- 16 unique symbols from `INDICES + COMMODITIES + HIGH_BETA`;
- `risk_modifier` values `1.0`, `0.5`, and `0.1`;
- CREDIT and DEBIT scenarios;
- 96 asserted rows in total;
- real cached OHLCV and production ATR14 computation through `cuttingboard.derived._compute`;
- real `QualificationResult`, `StructureResult`, and `DerivedMetrics` objects;
- the production `build_option_setups`.

All 48 CREDIT rows produced the same corrected ratio:

```text
strategy-aware total loss / charged total risk = 2.333333...
```

All 48 DEBIT rows produced:

```text
strategy-aware total loss / charged total risk = 1.000000
```

There were zero deviations by symbol, ATR14, or risk modifier.

For spread width `W`, the production debit estimate is `0.30W`. For a CREDIT spread, strategy-aware maximum loss is `W - 0.30W = 0.70W`. The continuation path charges `0.30W` instead. Therefore `strategy-aware / charged = 0.70W / 0.30W = 2.333333...` and `charged / strategy-aware = 0.428571...`. For a DEBIT spread, both the charged amount and strategy-aware maximum loss are the debit: `0.30W / 0.30W = 1.000000`.

The original retrieval script printed one intermediate ratio with mismatched units: total `OptionSetup.dollar_risk` divided by the per-share `_max_loss_for_strategy` result. This did not contaminate the underlying runtime data. Recomputing from the populated raw columns with consistent units produced the ratios above for every row.

Representative runtime rows:

```text
SPY CREDIT:  1 contract x $3.50 x 100 / $150.00 = 2.333333
SPY DEBIT:   1 contract x $1.50 x 100 / $150.00 = 1.000000
GLD CREDIT:  2 contracts x $1.75 x 100 / $150.00 = 2.333333
TSLA CREDIT: 1 contract x $1.75 x 100 / $75.00  = 2.333333
META CREDIT: 1 contract x $1.75 x 100 / $75.00  = 2.333333
```

The TSLA and META figures also confirm that the post-resize `OptionSetup.dollar_risk` must not be confused with the earlier qualification-layer ATR-derived risk figure.

One cache-provenance detail does not affect the result: MSTR's latest cached bar was dated 2026-07-06, while the other 15 symbols were current through 2026-07-09. Because the ratio follows directly from the two spread formulas and width cancels, this asynchronous refresh does not alter the conclusion.

Disposition: the 2.333x CREDIT understatement, 1.000x DEBIT result, and ATR/risk-modifier independence are reproduced.
