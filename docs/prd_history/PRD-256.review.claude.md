REQUIRED EDITS

1. Floor-regime symbol count is internally inconsistent and, where
   unqualified, wrong. Section 6 states "13 of 16 real tradable symbols are
   in this floor regime today" and section 8 states "a floor-regime CREDIT
   candidate (the common case — 13/16 real symbols)" with no qualifier.
   Independent recomputation of `proxy = max(0.50, atr14*0.05)` for all 16
   symbols shows exactly 11 are pinned at the literal $0.50 floor (XLE,
   PAAS, SLV, GDX, USO, IWM, NVDA, AMZN, GLD, AAPL, SPY); MSTR
   (atr14=10.209, proxy=0.510) and COIN (atr14=10.581, proxy=0.529) sit
   just above it (real/proxy ratios 3.90x and 3.73x respectively — still
   inside the stated "3x-9x" range, but not floor-pinned). Section 4's
   phrasing ("13 of 16 ... sit at or near the $0.50 floor") is defensible
   as written because of its "at or near" qualifier; sections 6 and 8 drop
   that qualifier and should either say "11" (strict floor) or be
   re-worded to match section 4's "at or near" framing consistently. Fix:
   align sections 6 and 8 to section 4's language, or state both counts
   explicitly (11 floor-pinned; 13 within a stated near-floor band) so a
   reader cannot take "13/16 floor regime" as a literal claim about the
   $0.50 pin.

2. Wrong line citation for the hardcoded direction. Section 1 states
   "Direction is hardcoded `\"LONG\"` (line 726)." Line 726 in the reviewed
   snapshot is a comment (part of the PRD-252 provenance note at lines
   721-728: "# main constant -- this path's ATR-based proxy still
   understates true"). The actual `direction="LONG"` assignment inside
   `_qualify_continuation_candidate` is at line 742. Fix: correct the
   citation to line 742.

3. Captured-run data-source description overstates its own coverage.
   Section 2, point 1 states captured production runs (`logs/run_*.json`,
   95 files) "each carries a `continuation_audit` block." Independent
   census of all 95 files found only 1 (`logs/run_2026-04-12_130000.json`)
   has a non-null `continuation_audit` key; the other 94 do not carry
   usable per-run data on this question. The "summed accepted across all
   95 files: 0" conclusion happens to still be correct (it sums to 0
   whether computed over 1 file or 95, since the other 94 contribute
   nothing either way), but the "each carries" framing is factually wrong,
   and DECISIONS.md's echo ("consistent with 95/95 captured production
   runs ... also showing zero accepted continuation candidates ever")
   overstates this specific data source's evidentiary weight — it reads as
   95 independent confirmations when only 1 file actually contains
   confirming data. Fix: correct "each carries a `continuation_audit`
   block" to state that only 1 of 95 files carries a non-null block, and
   soften the DECISIONS.md "95/95 ... showing zero" phrasing to avoid
   implying 95 independent per-run confirmations (the stronger, genuinely
   95-file-independent corroboration is the separate full historical
   replay in point 2, ~4,000 real symbol-days, which does hold up — see
   RATIONALE).

RECOMMENDED EDITS

1. Date-range off-by-one. Section 2 states the captured-run span is
   "2026-04-12..2026-07-09." The true max filename date across all 95
   files is 2026-07-10. Low materiality (no computed figure depends on
   this), but should be corrected in both the proposal doc and DECISIONS.md's
   echo of the same range.

2. Section 9's illustrative NVDA breakout-rate aside ("breakout fires
   36/225 times on NVDA in isolation") does not reproduce under the most
   direct reading of the code's own minimum-window guard (`need =
   CONTINUATION_BREAKOUT_BARS + 1 + max(1, CONTINUATION_HOLD_CANDLES) = 7`
   applied to NVDA's 255-row cache): independent recomputation gives
   39/249, not 36/225. This aside supports an already out-of-scope,
   explicitly-not-fixed-here finding (section 9) and does not feed any
   table or the R1 conclusion, so it is not blocking, but the document
   should either show the exact windowing method that produces 225 (if a
   different, undisclosed convention — e.g. an ATR warm-up exclusion — was
   used) or correct the figure to 39/249 for consistency with its own
   stated "the system's actual [function]" methodology.

3. Section 4's benchmark formula (`real_max_loss = width - spread_width`,
   treating the ATR proxy as playing "the same role `_estimated_debit`
   plays on the direct path") is adequately disclosed as a non-endorsed
   substitution, not a hidden mischaracterization of the actual code (the
   real direct-path formula is `strike_distance - _estimated_debit(
   strike_distance)` where `_estimated_debit = strike_distance * 0.30` —
   confirmed verbatim at `cuttingboard/options.py:434-458`). Consider one
   added clause making explicit that the two formulas differ in more than
   just the width source (the direct path applies a 30%-of-width discount
   before subtracting; this benchmark subtracts the raw ATR-derived proxy
   directly), so a future reader building on this table doesn't assume the
   arithmetic shapes are otherwise identical.

4. The 16 ATR14 readings are not all as-of the same cache date: 15 of 16
   symbols' caches end 2026-07-09, but MSTR's ends 2026-07-06. The document
   describes the table as computed against "`*_ohlcv.parquet` as of
   2026-07-10 cache refresh" without flagging this one symbol's staleness.
   Doesn't change any figure materially, but worth a footnote for a
   HIGH-RISK document.

5. Section 2 describes the 16 tradable-symbol caches as "~225-255 trading
   days each"; independent count shows all 16 are exactly 255 rows. Minor
   — tighten to "255" if that is uniformly true, rather than implying
   variation.
