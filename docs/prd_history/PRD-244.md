R1 — The decision (the spine of this PRD)
FINDING, verified at `331f950` (every cite below was read, not recalled):
1. Gate 6 enforces two stop-distance floors on the DIRECT candidate geometry: a percent-of-price leg (`stop_pct < MIN_STOP_PCT`, `qualification.py:387`, denominator `candidate.entry_price` at line 381) and an ATR leg (`risk < atr14 × STOP_ATR_FLOOR_K`, lines 382-396; `STOP_ATR_FLOOR_K = 1.0`, `config.py:113`). A candidate reaching the FVG upgrade has passed both on its pre-swap stop, with production risk pinned at exactly 1.0×ATR14 (`_build_candidate` sets `risk_distance = dm.atr14`, `options.py:362-368`).
2. `_resolve_entry_mode` then swaps the stop to a zone bound and the entry to the zone midpoint (`qualification.py:774-775`) and applies exactly one check to the new geometry: the R:R re-check (lines 777-780). `options.py:186-199` rewrites the traded candidate to that swapped geometry; the only stop references after the override are log lines (`options.py:201-204`; the `stop <= 0` check at 375-377 is upstream candidate construction). The floor is never applied to the stop that trades; Gate 6's recorded PASS refers to a stop that is discarded.
3. The one check that does re-fire rewards the failure mode: `imbalance_rr = reward / risk` (`qualification.py:777`) — a tighter stop raises the ratio, so the R:R re-check passes MORE easily precisely as the stop tightens. R:R is structurally incapable of policing stop distance; only a distance floor can.
4. Reachability of harm: post-swap risk = `gap_size / 2`. The only lower bound on the gap is `FVG_GAP_K × ATR14 = 0.3×ATR14` (`qualification.py:570-572`; `config.py:116`), so post-swap risk floors at 0.15×ATR14 — a 6.7× tightening below the 1.0×ATR14 floor Gate 6 just enforced. Any zone with `gap < 2.0×ATR14` tightens the traded stop below that floor. Widening is also reachable (no upper gap bound); the swap is bidirectional, but tighten-below-floor is reachable under current config. In VISION terms this is the invalidation level ("what invalidates it") landing inside the noise band Gate 6 exists to reject — on the FVG path only.
5. Unspecified, not merely undocumented: the escape from both floor legs appears in ZERO governing surfaces — `docs/trade_qualification.md` PULLBACK_IMBALANCE section documents only detection/proximity/R:R re-check (lines 343-363) and states "sizing and all gate results are unchanged from the DIRECT pass" (lines 362-363), i.e. it acknowledges the PASS refers to pre-swap geometry without addressing whether that is acceptable; PRD-007 R3 (PRD-007.md:44) mandates the zone-bound stop with no floor language; PRD-240 R4 covers only R:R tiering (PRD-240.md:69-73) and R6 covers only the continuation stop (PRD-240.md:81-86); the source audit flagged only the R:R branch on this path (`audits/qualification-tuning-2026-07-05/findings.md:280-297`); the `_resolve_entry_mode` body carries no comments at all (`qualification.py:750-786`). Contrast the continuation-stop asymmetry, which PRD-240 R6 documents in three places. The silence cannot be excused as "docs lagging code": PRD-241 (PR #113, `63c4aa4`) was the doc-truth pass and is the latest commit on these surfaces.

THE RULING: rule the swapped-stop floor escape into exactly one of two readings, on the record, with the reasoning written out. Do NOT default to "add the floor."

Branch A — Hole. The floor should re-fire on the swapped stop; FVG mode is currently laundering noise-width stops past a gate every other path obeys. → activates R2.

Branch B — Deliberate. The zone IS the risk definition; imposing a fixed ATR/percent floor on a structurally-anchored stop is a category error, and the structural bound is the correct invalidation regardless of width. → activates R3.

Trap to clear before choosing Branch B by analogy to R6: R6 (continuation stop) chose no-floor-plus-document, and the structural parallel is tempting. But R6's justification was a specific arithmetic — floor (1.0×ATR) + a fixed 3.0×ATR reward ceiling collapses the qualifying band to 0.5×ATR, a de facto path shutdown. That arithmetic does not transfer: the FVG reward is `target_price`-derived (`qualification.py:776`), not ATR-pinned, so flooring the FVG stop would not necessarily strangle the path. Branch B may be correct, but it must be argued on its own grounds and cannot borrow R6's shutdown reasoning. State the grounds explicitly.

FAIL: R1 resolves by adding the floor without recording why Branch B was rejected; or Branch B is justified by citing R6's shutdown arithmetic.

R2 — Branch A implementation (only if R1 = Hole)
1. Re-run BOTH floor legs against the post-swap geometry inside `_resolve_entry_mode` (or fail the upgrade back to the DIRECT result when the swapped stop is under-floor — decide reject-vs-fallback and record the choice in this PRD before implementing).
2. Use the correct denominator for the percent leg: the pre-swap `MIN_STOP_PCT` pass was measured against `candidate.entry_price` (`qualification.py:381`), but post-swap entry is the zone midpoint; `(gap/2)/midpoint` is currently computed nowhere. A Branch-A floor that reused the pre-swap denominator would be a second silent error. In scope for Branch A specifically because it is inseparable from applying the floor correctly.
3. Confirm Gate 6's recorded PASS in the qualification trace reflects the geometry that actually trades, or annotate the trace so the PASS cannot be read as covering the swapped stop.

FAIL: floor re-applied on only one leg; percent leg re-applied against the pre-swap entry denominator; trace still records an unannotated PASS that refers to a discarded stop.
