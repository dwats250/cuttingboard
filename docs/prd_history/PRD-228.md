CLASS
GOVERNANCE

WHY NOW
The chatgpt-codex-connector audit (2026-07-03) exercised the connector on live
PRs and exposed that CLAUDE.md records what does NOT satisfy the Codex gate
(ephemeral connector comments) but never states what MUST happen to a substantive
bot thread. Without the rule, a real catch can be silently ignored, silently
patched to clear a thread, or a resolved thread mistaken for a satisfied gate.
The audit itself was the forcing function.

MAX EXPECTED DELTA
One bullet (~20 lines) in CLAUDE.md § "Review and commit discipline". No source,
test, or other-file change.

GOAL
Codify how automated PR-reviewer (connector-bot) threads are dispositioned:
advisory input, never gate-satisfying, and never left dangling.

SCOPE
- Add one bullet to CLAUDE.md § "Review and commit discipline", directly after
  the existing connector-only-comment clause it extends.
- The bullet states: bot threads are advisory INPUT, never gate-satisfying; every
  substantive thread is ACTIONED (fix lands, thread resolved citing the SHA/PRD)
  or DISMISSED with a one-line in-thread reason; a real defect gets the normal
  treatment (PRD-before-build when non-trivial, mutation-verified red test); the
  thread never substitutes for the Claude review and any durable Codex
  cross-review that PRD's lane/CLASS actually requires.
- The bullet self-designates MANUAL-MERGE-ONLY, consistent with the existing
  governance carve-out.

OUT OF SCOPE
- Mechanical enforcement (CODEOWNERS / branch-protection). The clause is
  agent-honored policy like the rest of the carve-out; PRD-186 R4's CODEOWNERS
  hardening remains the separate, deferred mechanical path.
- Any change to what SATISFIES the Codex cross-review gate — that clause (the
  connector-only-comment properties) is unchanged; PRD-228 governs thread
  DISPOSITION only, and preserves the existing scoped Codex trigger.

CHANGE SURFACE
Mandatory (FILES intersects a GOVERNANCE HIGH-RISK FILE — CHANGE SURFACE Trigger
#2). CLAUDE.md: additive-only — a single new bullet inserted into § "Review and
commit discipline", immediately after the connector-only-comment clause. No
existing sentence, clause, or guardrail is edited, reordered, or removed; no
other section of CLAUDE.md is touched.

REQUIREMENTS

R1 — The disposition bullet exists with its three sub-points
CLAUDE.md carries a "Bot-review-thread disposition" bullet whose three sub-points
are (1) triage: action-or-dismiss in-thread, (2) a real defect gets normal
PRD/red-test treatment, (3) the thread is not the artifact.
FAIL: `grep -c "Bot-review-thread disposition" CLAUDE.md` returns 0, or any of
the three sub-points is absent.

R2 — Placement extends, not restates, the connector clause
The bullet sits under § "Review and commit discipline" immediately after the
connector-only-comment clause and references it ("per the clause above") rather
than re-deriving it.
FAIL: the bullet appears outside § "Review and commit discipline", or before the
connector-only-comment clause.

R3 — Additive-only; no existing guardrail mutated
The CLAUDE.md diff is a pure insertion; no pre-existing line in the file is
edited, reordered, or deleted.
FAIL: `git diff origin/main -- CLAUDE.md` shows any deleted or modified
pre-existing line (any `-` hunk line that is not part of the inserted block).

R4 — Codex-gate semantics unchanged; scoped trigger preserved
The bullet does not alter what satisfies the Codex cross-review gate, and its
sub-point 3 preserves the existing scoped trigger ("any durable Codex cross-review
that PRD's lane/CLASS actually requires"), not a universal one.
FAIL: the bullet states or implies that every HIGH-RISK PRD requires a durable
Codex cross-review, or edits the connector-only-comment gate clause.

DATA FLOW
connector bot posts inline thread → (advisory input) → triage: ACTIONED (fix +
cite SHA/PRD + resolve) | DISMISSED (one-line reason) → thread closed; the
Claude/Codex review artifacts remain the gate, not the thread.

FAIL CONDITIONS
- The disposition bullet or any of its three sub-points is absent (R1).
- The bullet is misplaced relative to § "Review and commit discipline" / the
  connector clause (R2).
- Any pre-existing CLAUDE.md line is mutated (R3).
- The bullet broadens or edits the Codex-gate trigger (R4).

VALIDATION
