SUMMARY
PRD-252 raises `config.MAX_RISK_PCT_PER_TRADE` from 0.01 to 0.026667
(effective per-trade budget $150 -> ~$400.005), leaves `ACCOUNT_EQUITY`
untouched, and decouples the EXPANSION-regime continuation path onto a new
`CONTINUATION_MAX_RISK_PCT_PER_TRADE = 0.01` constant at both call sites
that size a continuation-shaped `QualificationResult`
(`qualification.py::_qualify_continuation_candidate` and
`options.py::build_option_setups`'s correlation-modifier recompute). I
verified by direct repo grep that these are in fact the *only* two
production reads of `MAX_RISK_PCT_PER_TRADE` outside `config.py` itself
plus the untouched Gate-8 direct-path read (`qualification.py:428`), so the
PRD's "both sites, no others" claim is correct. Full suite
(2934 passed, 1 xfailed) and `ruff check` on the three changed production
files match the PRD's claimed results exactly; I ran both myself rather
than trusting the PRD text. The test-suite reconciliation (R2-R6a) is
faithful to its own stated rationale: PRD-251-era formula-selection
invariants are correctly monkeypatched to stay budget-independent, and
genuine budget-dependent consequences are recomputed to real, verified
values. The one substantive gap: the second continuation-path decouple
site (`options.py`'s `budget_pct` branch) — which the PRD's own prose
identifies as the harder-to-notice half of the fix, masked by a `min()`
clamp under high correlation modifiers — ships with no test that would
catch its removal. I verified this directly: reverting only that branch
(reintroducing the exact leak the PRD describes) leaves the full suite at
2934/2934 green with zero failures.

1. **R6's file:line citations for the two decouple sites are off by a few
   lines from the actual assignment statements.**
   PRD-252.md:163-164 and :205 cite `qualification.py:725` and
   `options.py:224` as the two sizing sites. At the reviewed commit, line
   725 in `qualification.py` is the first line of the PRD-252 explanatory
   comment; the actual `continuation_budget = ...` assignment is at line
   729. Line 224 in `options.py` is likewise inside the explanatory
   comment block; the actual `budget_pct = (...)` / `effective_risk = ...`
   lines are 232-237. The CHANGE SURFACE section (PRD-252.md:84, :89)
   correctly hedges these same two references with `~`, but R6 and FAIL
   CONDITIONS state them as exact line numbers. Low severity — the sites
   themselves are correctly identified and the surrounding comment makes
   them easy to find — but worth tightening so a future reader jumping
   straight to `qualification.py:725` or `options.py:224` lands on the
   commentary rather than the code it describes.

2. **The new `CONTINUATION_MAX_RISK_PCT_PER_TRADE` interim constant is
   acknowledged debt with no re-evaluation date in
   `docs/PROJECT_STATE.md`'s "Known technical debt" section.**
   VISION.md's operating principles state "Acknowledged debt carries a
   re-evaluation date... `docs/PROJECT_STATE.md` names the date each known
   debt is re-examined," and `docs/PROJECT_STATE.md`'s existing debt entry
   for the `runtime/` package split (`docs/PROJECT_STATE.md:107-111`)
   follows this pattern with an explicit "Re-evaluate by 2026-08-15." The
   new frozen constant is real, disclosed, tracked debt by the PRD's own
   framing (`docs/prd_history/PRD-251.continuation-path.proposal.md`'s new
   "TRACKED REQUIREMENT" section, and the PRD-252 DECISIONS.md entry) —
   but it lives only in the PRD-252 "Active PRD" bullet and the proposal
   doc's tracked-requirement note, neither of which is a dated
   re-evaluation commitment, and it will need to move out of "Active PRD"
   at close. Consider adding a "Known technical debt" bullet at close
   (or amending this PRD) naming a target date or an explicit "no date,
   blocked on the ATR-proxy fast-follow" disposition, so this doesn't
   silently read as permanent the way undated deferrals tend to in this
   codebase's own stated failure mode.
