# FOLLOW-UP — HEAD advanced e2bf916 → 7d74ae2 (advisory closed)

**What the delta does** (`git diff e2bf916 7d74ae2 -- cuttingboard/delivery/dashboard_renderer.py`):
the OUT line no longer renders `invalidation[0]` alone. It now appends the
non-redundant tail of `downgrade` — the substring after the literal `" or "`
(the structural-invalidation clause) — guarded against duplication:

```
        invalidation = entry.get("invalidation")
        if invalidation and len(invalidation) > 0 and invalidation[0] is not None:
            out_text = _esc(invalidation[0])
            downgrade = tf.get("downgrade")
            if downgrade and " or " in downgrade:
                structural = downgrade.split(" or ", 1)[1].strip()
                if structural and structural not in invalidation[0]:
                    out_text = f"{out_text}, or {_esc(structural)}"
            w(f'  <div class="label">OUT →</div><div class="value-key value-actionable">{out_text}</div>')
```

**Updated §4 finding for 7d74ae2 — PASS, but the character of OUT changed.** At
e2bf916 OUT was a pure single-field passthrough; at 7d74ae2 it is a
**composition of two existing fields** (`invalidation[0]` + the verbatim tail of
`downgrade`), joined with the connector `", or "`. It still introduces no
reworded, abbreviated, or synthesized condition wording — it splits on the
literal `" or "` and passes the tail substring through `_esc` verbatim, and the
`structural not in invalidation[0]` guard prevents re-duplicating the price
clause. This is a legitimate presentation-only compose (read two fields, join
for display) and it directly resolves the advisory: the one non-redundant datum
that dropping the RISK line would have lost is now surfaced on OUT. No logic,
grade, or lifecycle computation is touched.
