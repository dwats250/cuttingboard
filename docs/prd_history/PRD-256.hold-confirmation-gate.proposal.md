Fast-follow proposal — continuation-path HOLD-confirmation gate defect

Status: PROMOTED to PRD-259 (2026-07-14, Dustin's Gate A ruling — see
`docs/prd_history/PRD-259.md` and docs/DECISIONS.md 2026-07-14). The
ruled direction is the OPEN DESIGN QUESTION's option 1: shift the
lookback back by `hold_candles` so `breakout_level` is computed from
bars strictly prior to the bar under test. PRD-259 R3's real-data
characterization of the corrected gate is appended at the end of this
document.

ORIGIN
Surfaced during PRD-256 Phase 1/R1's real-data validation (2026-07-13),
replaying the real, unmodified `_qualify_continuation_candidate` against
real cached OHLCV/VIX data to find a real accepted continuation candidate
to walk through. None existed to find — this proposal documents why.

THE FINDING
`cuttingboard/qualification.py::detect_continuation_breakout` (lines
623-639) computes `breakout_level` as the max High over the 5 bars *up
to and including* yesterday (`lookback = df.iloc[-(n+1):-1]`, `n=5`, on
the frame ending today). `_qualify_continuation_candidate`'s HOLD check
(lines 669-673) then asks whether *yesterday's own close*
(`hold_close = df.iloc[-(hold_candles+1)]`, `hold_candles=1`, i.e.
`df.iloc[-2]`) already exceeded that same window's max — but position -2
sits *inside* the lookback window (`-6` to `-2` inclusive) that computed
`breakout_level` in the first place, and the code rejects on
`hold_close <= breakout_level` (equality rejects; passage requires
*strict* `>`). This is not merely self-referential, and there is no
degenerate escape: for any valid OHLC bar, `High >= Close` by
construction — so yesterday's own High is always `>=` yesterday's Close
(`hold_close`), and `breakout_level` (the max High over a window that
includes yesterday's own bar) is therefore always `>=` yesterday's own
High, hence always `>= hold_close`. `hold_close <= breakout_level` is
consequently a mathematical certainty for any real market data, not a
near-certainty with a narrow escape — even the case where yesterday
closed exactly at its own high, and that high was the window's maximum,
still fails, because the rejection test is `<=` (equality included), not
`<`. **`NO_HOLD_CONFIRMATION` cannot be avoided by any valid OHLC input
given `CONTINUATION_HOLD_CANDLES = 1`; it is not merely "nearly"
unsatisfiable, it is provably always triggered.** (An earlier version of
this document, and the commissioned second-model disposition reviewing
it, both described a "degenerate equality case" as a theoretical escape
— that description was itself imprecise: equality is on the rejecting
side of the `<=` test, not the passing side, so no such case exists.
Corrected here, caught by `chatgpt-codex-connector[bot]`'s continued
review of PR #145.) The `High >= Close` premise is verified against real
market data, not merely assumed: across all 5,985 rows in the real
cached OHLCV (`data/cache/*.parquet`, all 23 cached symbols), zero rows
violate `High < Close`, and 120 rows hit the exact-equality case
(`High == Close`) — every one of those 120 real, observed degenerate
rows lands on the rejecting side of the `<=` test, so the real data
contains no counterexample to the proof above (independently confirmed
by `docs/prd_history/PRD-256.review.claude.v3.md`).

This appears inconsistent with the documented spec
(`docs/trade_qualification.md:323`, gate 4: "Close 1 completed bar ago
also held above the breakout level," constant `CONTINUATION_HOLD_CANDLES
= 1`) — the prose implies an *earlier* breakout level (established
before the hold candle) than the one the current bar's breakout check
just set, not a same-window self-comparison.

EVIDENCE — stated at the strength the data actually supports, not
overstated
The load-bearing evidence is a full historical replay of the real,
unmodified `_qualify_continuation_candidate`/`detect_continuation_breakout`
against real cached OHLCV for all 16 real tradable symbols (`SPY QQQ IWM`
+ `GLD SLV GDX PAAS USO XLE` + `NVDA TSLA AAPL META AMZN COIN MSTR`, 255
trading days each — 3,984 real symbol-days total), with the VIX-timing
gate held favorable to isolate the HOLD question specifically. Result:
breakout fired 680 times; HOLD passed **zero** times, across every
symbol, every day, with no exception.

Two corroborating (not confirming) sources were also checked and do not
contradict this:
- **95 captured production runs** (`logs/run_*.json`, 2026-04-12 to
  2026-07-10). Only 1 of the 95 carries a non-null `continuation_audit`
  block (`total_candidates=2, accepted=0`); the other 94 contain no
  usable `continuation_audit` data — precisely, 92 carry the key with an
  empty/null value and 2 omit the key entirely (independently confirmed
  by the commissioned second-model disposition,
  `docs/prd_history/PRD-256.review.codex.md`) — which is absence of data,
  not a confirming zero. Do not read this as "95/95 runs confirm"; that
  framing overstates what 94 of the 95 files actually show.
- **`logs/audit.jsonl`** (640 total records: 635 notification-transport,
  5 genuine pipeline records per `docs/audit_doctrine.md`'s two-family
  structure). None of the 5 pipeline records carries a non-null
  `continuation_audit` block either — also silent, not confirming.

**Does the conclusion hold on this honest evidence?** Yes. The
conclusion — the continuation path has functionally never qualified a
candidate, consistent with this gate defect — rests entirely on the
3,984-symbol-day replay, which is independent of and does not need the
log-count framing to stand. The overstated "95/95 confirm" phrasing was
never load-bearing for this conclusion; removing it changes nothing
about whether the finding is real.

WHY THIS IS NOT A DROP-IN FIX
Correcting the self-reference requires deciding what "held above the
breakout level" should actually compare — e.g. re-deriving
`breakout_level` from a window that excludes the hold candle(s), or
re-anchoring the comparison to a differently-shifted lookback. That is a
modeling decision about the intended confirmation semantics, not a
one-line arithmetic fix, and needs its own Gate A before any build (same
posture PRD-251's continuation-path proposal took toward the width
question, and PRD-256 R1's own text toward the FIX-approach choice).

OPEN DESIGN QUESTION (needs its own Gate A before any build)
What should "held above the breakout level" mean when
`CONTINUATION_HOLD_CANDLES = 1`? Not evaluated, not ranked:
1. Compute `breakout_level` from a window that ends *before* the hold
   candle(s) (e.g. shift the lookback back by `hold_candles`), so the
   hold check compares against a level the candidate could plausibly
   have already cleared.
2. Redefine "hold" as confirming today's breakout persisted through
   yesterday's own high/close relative to the *prior* (pre-breakout-day)
   structure, decoupling it from `detect_continuation_breakout`'s window
   entirely.
3. Drop the HOLD gate's dependency on `detect_continuation_breakout`'s
   window and give it an independently-derived reference level.

RELATIONSHIP TO PRD-256
Same function (`_qualify_continuation_candidate`), a different gate
(HOLD, gate 4 — a qualification precondition) than PRD-256's ATR-proxy
finding (sizing, the final gate before acceptance). Independent defects
in the same code path: this gate defect is why PRD-256's sizing
understatement has had zero real-world trades to date, but the sizing
bug would fire on literally the first candidate that ever clears HOLD,
including under today's decoupled $150 cap. Not folded into PRD-256's
R3 (FIX) scope — needs its own PRD number and its own Gate A.

FILES LIKELY IN SCOPE (estimate only, not binding — re-derive at Stage 0)
`cuttingboard/qualification.py` (`detect_continuation_breakout`,
`_qualify_continuation_candidate`), `tests/test_continuation_mode.py`,
`docs/trade_qualification.md` (gate 4's documented semantics).

## PRD-259 R3 — characterization of the CORRECTED gate (2026-07-14)

What the gate accepts once it can fire, measured before merge. Method:
the real, unmodified `_qualify_continuation_candidate` (post-PRD-259
window) replayed day-by-day over the full real cached OHLCV
(`data/cache/*_ohlcv.parquet`, 2025-07-07..2026-07-13) for all 16
tradable symbols, 3,615 eligible symbol-days per run, real Wilder ATR14
(`cuttingboard.derived._wilder_atr`), real EMA21, real day-over-day VIX
for the VIX gate, real VIX level for IV-environment classification,
fixed 10:00 ET clock (TIME gate passes). Accepted candidates were then
run through the real, unmodified `options.py::build_option_setups` at
the PRD-256 re-converged budget (`ACCOUNT_EQUITY x
MAX_RISK_PCT_PER_TRADE` = $400.005). Replay caveats, same as PRD-256
R1's: structure classification (CHOP exclusion) and the
EXPANSION-regime precondition are NOT replayed - this isolates the
continuation gate sequence, holding structure TREND; real acceptance
frequency in production is strictly LOWER than these figures because
EXPANSION must also hold.

### 1. Pre-fix control: the harness reproduces the impossibility

The pre-fix `_qualify_continuation_candidate` (loaded verbatim from git
commit `947e15c`, the stage-0 commit) over the identical harness:
**0 acceptances** in 3,615 symbol-days under real VIX, **0** with the
VIX gate held favorable. The pre-fix funnel contains ONLY
`NO_BREAKOUT`/`VIX_BLOCKED`/`NO_HOLD_CONFIRMATION` - no candidate ever
reached the momentum gate, so every gate downstream of HOLD fires for
the first time under the corrected window.

### 2. Acceptance frequency and funnel (corrected gate)

| run | accepted | rate | funnel (rejections) |
|---|---|---|---|
| real VIX | 21 / 3,615 | 0.58% | NO_BREAKOUT 1625, VIX_BLOCKED 1439, NO_HOLD 272, MOMENTUM 221, EXTENDED 28, STOP_TOO_TIGHT 7, RR 2 |
| VIX held favorable | 32 / 3,615 | 0.89% | NO_BREAKOUT 2777, NO_HOLD 371, MOMENTUM 357, EXTENDED 60, STOP_TOO_TIGHT 16, RR 2 |

~21 acceptances per year across the whole 16-symbol board (~1.75 per
month) BEFORE the un-replayed EXPANSION-regime precondition thins it
further. Not a floodgate; the gate sequence stays selective - HOLD
still rejects 272 breakouts (real VIX), momentum another 221.

### 3. Concentration - none suspicious

By symbol (10 of 16 represented, max 4): GDX 4, IWM 3, XLE 3, NVDA 3,
AMZN 2, COIN 2, PAAS/TSLA/META/MSTR 1 each. SPY/QQQ/GLD/SLV/USO/AAPL: 0.

1-2. By IV environment: NORMAL_IV 15, LOW_IV 4, ELEVATED_IV 2. No
regime, symbol, or structure cluster dominates.

### 4. Anatomy - the acceptances look like the documented trade

All 21: entry = close above a 5-bar consolidation high that the hold
candle had already closed above. Risk (entry - breakout stop) spans
0.27-1.49x ATR14 (the RR gate's 1.5x ceiling binds exactly as designed:
2 rejections sit just past it); extension from EMA21 spans 0.16-2.48x
ATR (2.5x cap binds: 28 rejections). Hand-verified example, IWM
2025-11-25: five sessions consolidating under 237.31 (11-17..11-21);
11-24 closes 238.11 above it (hold); 11-25 closes 243.30 (entry), stop
237.31, risk $5.98/sh = 1.17x ATR, RR 2.56. Pre-fix, the window
included 11-24's own high (238.59), so its close (238.11) could never
clear it. These are plausible momentum-continuation entries a
discretionary trader would recognize; nothing that looks like noise
acceptance.

The 21/3,615 figure conditions on EXPANSION + TREND structure being
true every day, which they are not; live acceptance will be rarer. The
VIX gate blocked 1,439 symbol-days (~40%) under real VIX - the
dominant environmental filter after breakout detection itself. No
option-chain data exists in-repo, so $-figures inherit the
`_estimated_debit` 30%-of-width convention's accuracy (unverified
