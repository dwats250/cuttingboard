WHY NOW
A skipped IWM/BTC-USD vote shrinks the confidence denominator
(regime.py: confidence = |net| / votes_cast), so dropout concentrates
confidence and can lift STAY_FLAT to a tradable posture on identical
surviving evidence. Verified instances: sol F1 (PRD-262 SECOND-MODEL,
evidence exhibit 2: IWM absent flips 0.500/STAY_FLAT to
0.571/CONTROLLED_LONG through the real compute_regime) and the FABLE
addendum arithmetic (4 RISK_ON + 2 NEUTRAL: 8 votes -> 0.50/STAY_FLAT,
6 votes -> 0.667/CONTROLLED_LONG). The Stage-0 replay over the PRD-262
cached window (247 complete-data days) measured the conditional impact:
a single silent IWM outage changes the emitted verdict on 49/217 vote
days (10 of them no-trade -> CONTROLLED_LONG) — the LIVE motivation,
since an IWM outage publishes its verdict. A BTC-USD outage changes the
vote-layer verdict on 42/217 days too (18 -> CONTROLLED_LONG, 8 ->
DEFENSIVE_SHORT), but those never reach a human: contract.py:527
_build_macro_drivers requires the bitcoin driver (BTC-USD) and errors
the run before any verdict or coverage reason publishes, pre- and
post-263 alike (see REALIZABILITY). The BTC figure is a vote-layer
count, not a published-outcome count. PRD-259/260 made EXPANSION-gated
promotion live and PRD-262 widened the dropout trigger population; the
vote model is the remaining aggregate that loosens under absence.

MAX EXPECTED DELTA
~15 production LOC across 2 files (regime.py, qualification.py).

GOAL
A skipped vote can never make the emitted regime verdict more
permissive, and below the derived coverage floor (7 cast votes for any
tradable posture; 6 cast votes can never trade) the system stands down
with a coverage-naming reason instead of answering from thin evidence.

GATE A RULING (Dustin, 2026-07-18 — build to it)
- Policy: worst-case bounding. k = 8 - votes_cast; bounded_net =
  sign(net) * max(0, |net| - k); confidence = |bounded_net| / 8;
  regime and posture classified from the bounded values. CHAOTIC check
  stays FIRST and unchanged. EXPANSION branch untouched — it returns
  total_votes=0 / confidence=1.0 by fiat, so the floor lives in the
  vote-model branch only, keyed on missing vote inputs, NEVER on
  total_votes.
- Refusal semantics: STAY_FLAT with a coverage-naming reason. No new
  regime/posture literal. No HALT, no new HaltCause. (A new literal
  fails open toward TRADABLE at ~8 consumer sites: contract
  tradable/status, hourly qualification gate, REGIME_RISK_MULTIPLIER
  default 1.0, execution-policy blocks, notification bias copy,
  postmarket scoring — Stage-0 consumer sweep.)
- Reason surfacing: yes — _check_regime_gates appends the coverage note
  into the reason that flows to system_state.stay_flat_reason. The
  string is for humans; the machine-filterable key is the truthful
  total_votes. NO new field.

SCOPE
1. cuttingboard/regime.py compute_regime (vote branch only): after the
   vote tally, bound net toward zero by the number of missing votes
   (clamped at zero — never cross sign), compute confidence over the
   structural 8-vote denominator, and classify regime + posture from
   the bounded values. Vote-count fields (risk_on_votes,
   risk_off_votes, neutral_votes, total_votes, net_score,
   vote_breakdown) stay the actual cast values. vix_level /
   vix_pct_change population is untouched (kill-switch feed). The
   docstring claim "degrades gracefully without halting" is rewritten
   to describe worst-case bounding (docs-match-code). The regime info
   log line gains the cast-vote count.
2. cuttingboard/qualification.py _check_regime_gates: when the Gate-1
   STAY_FLAT reason is built and 0 < regime.total_votes < 8, append a
   coverage note naming votes cast (e.g. "; confidence bounded
   worst-case at 7/8 votes cast"). Full-coverage reason strings are
   byte-identical to today.

Derived floor (arithmetic, not a knob): with unit votes in {-1,0,+1}
(verified: regime.py counts votes +1/-1/0; VIX's two votes are each
unit-weight) and posture thresholds conf >= 0.55 (CONTROLLED_LONG /
DEFENSIVE_SHORT) and >= 0.75 (AGGRESSIVE_LONG), bounding yields: at 7
cast votes a tradable posture requires net_cast >= 6 (bounded 5 ->
0.625) and AGGRESSIVE_LONG requires unanimity (7 -> bounded 6 -> 0.750,
the pre-existing exact >= 0.75 boundary that full-coverage net 6
already sits on); at 6 cast votes bounded confidence caps at 4/8 = 0.50
< 0.55, so no assignment of 6 votes reaches a tradable posture. The
floor emerges from the existing 0.55 boundary; no new constant exists
to drift.

compute_regime (vote branch only) computes k = 8 - votes_cast,
bounded_net = sign(net_cast) * max(0, |net_cast| - k), confidence =
|bounded_net| / 8, and classifies regime + posture from bounded_net and

R2 — Dropout can never out-permit (exhaustive invariant)
For every one of the 3^8 realizable full-coverage vote assignments,
removing IWM, BTC-USD, or both never yields a (regime, posture) more
permissive than the full-data verdict, under the explicit
permissiveness lattice: {CHAOTIC-or-STAY_FLAT posture}=0 <
NEUTRAL_PREMIUM=1 < {CONTROLLED_LONG, DEFENSIVE_SHORT}=2 <
{AGGRESSIVE_LONG, EXPANSION_LONG}=3. The lattice places CHAOTIC and
STAY_FLAT at least-permissive explicitly so the test cannot pass
vacuously.

FAIL: any assignment where rank(posture after dropout) >
rank(posture on full data). (The FABLE case — 4 RISK_ON + 2 NEUTRAL at
6 cast vs the same evidence plus 2 NEUTRAL at 8 — is one instance and
is also pinned as its own named test; red against current main:
0.667/CONTROLLED_LONG out-permits 0.500/STAY_FLAT.)

R3 — Structural floor at 6 cast votes; zero-clamp
No assignment of 6 cast votes (IWM and BTC-USD both absent) reaches a
tradable posture: all 3^6 assignments yield posture STAY_FLAT.
Bounding clamps at zero and never crosses sign: net_cast = +1 at 6 cast
votes classifies NEUTRAL with confidence 0.0, not RISK_OFF.

FAIL: any 6-cast assignment yields a posture other than STAY_FLAT; or
the net_cast=+1/k=2 case yields regime RISK_OFF or nonzero confidence.

R4 — Truthful counts; VIX fields populated under bounding
risk_on_votes, risk_off_votes, neutral_votes, total_votes, net_score,
and vote_breakdown remain the actual cast values (total_votes == 7
with one symbol absent; net_score is the cast net, not the bounded
net). vix_level and vix_pct_change are populated whenever ^VIX is
present in valid_quotes, on every bounding path — nulling them would
disarm the kill switch's VIX arms (runtime _kill_switch reads only
these two fields plus SPY).

FAIL: with IWM and BTC-USD absent and ^VIX present, the returned state

net_score != the cast net.

R5 — Coverage-naming reason
